## FATF Trade-Based Money Laundering Typologies

Trade-based money laundering indicators include repeated reuse of similar invoices,
misrepresentation of underlying trade activity, unusual pricing or volume patterns,
and circular movement of goods or receivables through connected parties.

In a supply chain finance setting, warning indicators include:
- multiple financing attempts for substantially similar invoices,
- supplier rings with repeated reciprocal trade links,
- cascades of receivables that do not align with documented delivery events,
- recently formed counterparties requesting disproportionate financing.

Where these indicators are present, institutions should document the rationale,
preserve evidence, and consider suspicious activity escalation.
